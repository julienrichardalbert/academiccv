bio

I am a post-doctoral researcher in the team of Sandra Duharcourt, figuring out how genes turn on and off. Controlling gene activity is fundamental to how the DNA programme unpacks and reads itself, ultimately giving rise to "endless forms most beautiful".
  I currently use computers to analyse and visualise complex quantitative biological data. Increasingly, I'm incorporating data from across the tree of life to study the evolution of genome regulatory systems.
  I led the bioinformatic analyses that uncovered non-canonical roles of DNA methylation in gene activation and chromatin looping in the Max Greenberg team.
  I completed my PhD with Matt Lorincz, where I developed and applied software to trace the inheritance of DNA modifications on maternal and paternal chromosomes.
  I also trained as a yeast molecular biologist accross many labs in my hometown of Montréal with J. Kornblatt, P. Joyce, R. Storms, M. Whiteway (F. Tebbji), and M. Tyers (A. Sellam).
